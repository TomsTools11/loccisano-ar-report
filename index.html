Qualification Bottleneck
Primary Issue Identified
The biggest bottleneck right now is qualification, not lead quality. Here's what's happening:
• Incidents: Leads with 2+ accidents/tickets are automatically disqualified by Allstate's underwriting. This is the #1 issue.
• Non-standard policies: Consumers carrying indemnity policies (Geico Indemnity, State Farm Indemnity, Progressive Indemnity) are considered non-standard and can't be written.
• Prior insurance gaps: Some leads lack 6 months of continuous prior coverage.
• Consumer honesty: 100% of leads so far indicated "no accidents" on the form, but some had incidents when quoted. We can't fully prevent this.
Action: We're adding a bid-down modifier for consumers who self-report accidents, and investigating if we can filter non-standard carriers.
✓

• If a lead asks for a quote over text, the team requires a phone call before quoting

Our Recommendations
Here are the 4 actions we recommend to improve your results
1
New Process: When a Lead Asks for a Quote Over Text
HIGHEST PRIORITY
When a lead texts asking for a quote, don't say no and don't just send it. Instead, say yes, then redirect to your process. The response should be something like: "We'd love to get you a quote! The first thing we need to do is grab a few details from you. Are you free for a quick call now, or let me know when works best for you." You're agreeing to give them what they want while steering the conversation to a phone call where you can qualify and quote properly. This keeps the lead engaged instead of losing them with a flat "no."
2
Target Your Highest-Closing Zip Codes With a Bid Modifier
HIGH PRIORITY
You're sending us your highest-closing zip codes based on your own book of business (primarily Erie and upstate New York, plus one Brooklyn zip). We'll apply a 20% bid increase on those zip codes as a starting point so you compete more aggressively for leads in the areas where you already have the best close rates. Your broader campaign stays at the current bid to maintain fulfillment, while the targeted zips get a boost. We'll monitor the distribution and adjust from there.
3
Capture Early Morning Volume With an Automated Email Responder
CAMPAIGN OPTIMIZATION
36 of your 43 leads came in during the 6–8 AM window, but your office doesn't open until 9. We're building you an automated email responder that triggers the moment a lead comes in outside of office hours. It will send a personalized email: "Hi [First Name], thanks for reaching out! We open at 9 AM and I'd love to help you. Feel free to grab a time on my calendar here: [link]." This keeps the lead warm until Vanessa can follow up. We'll also use this during lunchtime when leads tend to sit for ~40 minutes.
4
Give It Time: 15 Days Is Too Early to Judge ROI
PERSPECTIVE
The average auto insurance quote takes 3–7 days to close. Some consumers need 2–3 weeks to make a decision, especially when comparing multiple carriers. At day 15 with 43 leads, many of your quoted prospects may still be in the decision phase. We typically evaluate ROI at the 60–90 day mark when enough leads have had time to move through the full sales cycle. The leading indicators (low CPL, high conversion, targeted leads) are all strong.
